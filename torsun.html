Jays' Josh Donaldson leaves game at Fenway with injury

BOSTON – As if giving up five runs in the bottom of the fourth wasn’t bad enough, the Blue Jays got even more bad news in the next inning when star third baseman Josh Donaldson left the game with an apparent injury.

After drawing a walk, Donaldson pulled up noticeably while running to second base on a Justin Smoak double. He was able to jog into third but Jays trainer Nikki Huffman came out immediately and Donaldson was removed from the game.

It was the latest blow for a struggling Jays team in general but for Donaldson specifically. It’s been a rough year for Donaldson health wise. He missed 18 games earlier due to shoulder inflammation and has struggled with arm strength.

Donaldson was replaced on Monday by Gio Urshela, who came in as a pinch runner and remained at third.

The team later reported that Donaldson was suffering from tightness in his left calf and will be further evaluated to determine the extent of the injury.

The Jays opened a three-game series against the American League East leading Red Sox who had an 11-game lead on Toronto prior to the start. It’s been a brutal may so far for the Jays, who were 9-16 interring Monday’s play.
